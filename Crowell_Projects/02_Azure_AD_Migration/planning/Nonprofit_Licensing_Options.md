### Eligible Organizations

To qualify for Microsoft nonprofit offers, organizations must meet the following criteria:

âœ… **Qualifying Organization Types:**
- Nonprofit or non-governmental organization with recognized legal status equivalent to **501(c)(3)** status under the United States Internal Revenue Code
- Public library that provides general library services without charge
- Public museum that is open to the public

âœ… **Mission Requirements:**
- Must operate on a **not-for-profit basis**
- Must have a mission to **benefit the local community**, including:
  - Providing relief to the poor
  - Advancing education
  - Improving social welfare
  - Preserving culture
  - Promoting human rights

### Ineligible Organizations

âŒ **NOT Eligible for Nonprofit Licensing:**
- Governmental organizations or agencies
- Educational institutions (K-12 schools, colleges, universities)
- Healthcare organizations (hospitals, healthcare networks, health plans)
- Professional and trade associations
- Professional sports organizations
- Political, labor, and fraternal organizations

**Note:** Crowell Memorial Home, as a nonprofit continuing care retirement community (501(c)(3) status), **IS ELIGIBLE** for Microsoft nonprofit programs.

### User License Restrictions

**Nonprofit Grants (FREE licenses):**
- Permitted **ONLY** for:
  - Paid nonprofit employees
  - Unpaid executive staff

**Nonprofit Discounts (Discounted licenses):**
- Permitted for:
  - All nonprofit staff
  - Volunteers

**NOT Eligible:**
- Program participants
- Members
- Donors
- Residents (in the case of Crowell Memorial Home)

**Important:** For Crowell, licenses should be assigned to **staff only** (administrative, nursing, dietary, activities, etc.), **NOT residents**.

### How to Upgrade Windows 11 Home to Pro

#### Option 1: TechSoup Donated Licenses (Recommended)

**Process:**
1. Apply for TechSoup nonprofit validation (if not already validated)
2. Request Windows 11 Pro licenses through TechSoup
3. Receive product keys via email
4. Use product keys to upgrade Windows 11 Home to Pro

**Key Details:**
- **Cost:** $16 per license (administrative fee)
- **Quantity:** Up to 50 licenses every 2 years
- **Includes:** Downgrade rights to Windows 10 Pro
- **License Type:** Full license (NOT an upgrade-only license)
- **Can be used for:** Fresh installs OR upgrading from Home to Pro

**Upgrade Process:**
1. On Windows 11 Home device, go to **Settings > System > Activation**
2. Click **Change product key**
3. Enter the Windows 11 Pro product key from TechSoup
4. Windows will automatically upgrade from Home to Pro
5. Device will reboot and activate as Windows 11 Pro

**Important:** Microsoft provides **full Windows 11 Pro licenses** (not separate upgrade licenses) to nonprofits. A full Pro license can be used to upgrade a Home edition device.

#### Option 2: Microsoft 365 Business Premium Upgrade Rights

If you license users with **Microsoft 365 Business Premium**, they receive **Windows 11 Pro upgrade rights** as part of their subscription.

**How it works:**
- User must be assigned a Microsoft 365 Business Premium license
- Device must be running Windows 11 Home (or Windows 10 Home)
- Device must be Azure AD-joined or Hybrid Azure AD-joined
- Upgrade to Pro happens automatically via subscription activation

**Cost:** Included with Microsoft 365 Business Premium (~$5.50/user/month nonprofit)

**Benefit:** No need to purchase separate Windows licenses if you're already using Business Premium.

#### Option 3: Volume Licensing (Advanced)

Some nonprofits have reported success using **Microsoft Volume Licensing** to obtain Pro upgrade licenses, but this requires:
- Existing nonprofit volume license agreement with Microsoft
- Contacting Microsoft support for assistance
- May not be available in all regions

**Note:** There are conflicting reports about whether volume licensing can be used for Home to Pro upgrades. Some nonprofits report being quoted Â£275 (~$350 USD) for upgrades, while others have received free volume licenses with Microsoft support's help.

## Important 2025 License Changes

### July 1, 2025: End of FREE Business Premium & E1 Grants

**What's Changing:**

On **July 1, 2025**, Microsoft will discontinue offering **FREE donated licenses** for:
- âŒ Microsoft 365 Business Premium
- âŒ Office 365 E1

**What's Staying:**

âœ… **Microsoft 365 Business Basic** - Remains FREE (up to 300 users)

âœ… **Discounted pricing** (60-75% off) continues for:
- Microsoft 365 Business Premium (~$5.50/user/month instead of free)
- Office 365 E1 (~$2/user/month instead of free)
- All other nonprofit plans

**Impact on Existing Customers:**

- Organizations currently using **FREE Business Premium or E1 grants** can continue until their **next renewal date**
- Upon renewal **on or after July 1, 2025**, they must transition to:
  - **Option 1:** Paid (but discounted 75%) Business Premium or E1
  - **Option 2:** Downgrade to FREE Business Basic (lose desktop Office apps, MFA, Intune)
  - **Option 3:** Switch to alternative plans

**Timeline for Crowell Memorial Home:**

Since Crowell is **starting fresh** (not currently using Microsoft 365 grants), this change has **minimal impact**. However, it's important to understand:

- You will **NOT** receive FREE Business Premium grants (that ship has sailed as of May 2025)
- You **WILL** receive **discounted pricing** (75% off) for Business Premium: ~$5.50/user/month
- You **CAN** use FREE Business Basic for users who don't need desktop Office apps or advanced security

### April 1, 2025: Billing Model Changes

Starting **April 1, 2025**, Microsoft is changing billing options:

- **Annual Prepaid** (default) - Pay for full year upfront (best value)
- **Annual with Monthly Billing** - Pay monthly but commit to annual contract (+5% premium)
- **Monthly Billing** - Pay monthly with no commitment (+20% premium)

**Recommendation:** Choose **Annual Prepaid** to avoid premium charges and lock in lowest pricing.

### July 1, 2026: Commercial Price Increases

Microsoft announced that **commercial pricing will increase on July 1, 2026**. Nonprofit pricing will increase proportionally since it's tied to commercial pricing through fixed discount percentages (60-75%).

**Impact:** Nonprofit pricing will rise, but the **discount percentage remains the same** (60-75% off commercial).

## How to Apply & Get Started

### Step 1: Verify Nonprofit Eligibility

**TechSoup Validation** (Microsoft's official nonprofit validation partner)

1. **Create TechSoup Account:** https://www.techsoup.org/
2. **Submit Nonprofit Documentation:**
   - IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter
   - Organization's legal name and EIN (Employer Identification Number)
   - Proof of nonprofit status
3. **Validation Timeline:** Typically 3-7 business days

**Note:** Crowell Memorial Home, as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit continuing care retirement community, should easily qualify.

### Step 2: Register with Microsoft Nonprofit Portal

**Microsoft for Nonprofits:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/nonprofits

1. **Sign up** using your organization's email address
2. **Verify nonprofit status** through TechSoup integration
3. **Access nonprofit offers** once validated

### Step 3: Choose Licensing Path

**Option A: Direct from Microsoft (Recommended for 365/Entra ID)**
- Purchase Microsoft 365 and Entra ID licenses directly through Microsoft nonprofit portal
- Automatic nonprofit pricing applied
- Monthly or annual billing options

**Option B: Through Cloud Solution Provider (CSP)**
- Purchase through Microsoft partner/reseller (e.g., Pax8, CDW, Connection, Ciracom Cloud)
- CSP manages licenses and billing
- May offer additional support and services

**Option C: Through TechSoup (Recommended for Windows 11 Pro)**
- Best for Windows operating system licenses
- $16 per Windows 11 Pro license (up to 50 every 2 years)
- Request licenses through TechSoup portal after validation

### Step 4: Acquire Licenses

#### For Microsoft 365 & Entra ID:
1. Log in to **Microsoft 365 Admin Center** (admin.microsoft.com)
2. Navigate to **Billing > Purchase Services**
3. Select **Nonprofit offers**
4. Choose desired plans (e.g., Microsoft 365 Business Premium)
5. Enter number of licenses (59 users for Crowell)
6. Complete purchase

#### For Windows 11 Pro:
1. Log in to **TechSoup** (www.techsoup.org)
2. Search for "**Windows 11 Professional**"
3. Request **up to 50 licenses** (enough for Crowell's 68 computers - may need to request in 2 cycles)
4. Pay $16 administrative fee per license
5. Receive product keys via email within 3-5 business days
6. Use keys to upgrade Windows 11 Home devices to Pro

### Step 5: Assign and Deploy

1. **Assign licenses** to users in Microsoft 365 Admin Center
2. **Users receive welcome emails** with sign-in instructions
3. **Deploy Windows 11 Pro upgrades** using product keys
4. **Configure MFA** for all users (HIPAA requirement)
5. **Set up Intune** for device management

## Appendix: Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Does Crowell Memorial Home qualify as a nonprofit for Microsoft licensing?

**A:** Yes, absolutely. Crowell Memorial Home is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit continuing care retirement community, which qualifies for Microsoft nonprofit programs.

### Q: Can we license residents with nonprofit licenses?

**A:** No. Nonprofit licenses are only for **staff** (paid employees and unpaid executives). Residents are considered "program participants" and are not eligible.

### Q: What happens if we don't apply for nonprofit status?

**A:** You would pay **commercial pricing** (4x higher):
- Commercial: $19,824/year
- Nonprofit: $3,894/year
- **Lost savings: $15,930/year**

### Q: Do we need to renew our nonprofit validation annually?

**A:** TechSoup validation is typically valid for **2 years**, after which you may need to revalidate. Microsoft nonprofit status is ongoing once validated.

### Q: Can we use Windows 11 Pro licenses from TechSoup to upgrade Home devices?

**A:** Yes! The $16 licenses from TechSoup are **full licenses** that can be used for fresh installs OR to upgrade from Home to Pro. Simply enter the product key in Settings > Activation > Change product key.

### Q: What if we already have some Windows 10 Pro devices?

**A:** Windows 10 Pro devices can be upgraded to Windows 11 Pro for **free** if they meet hardware requirements. You don't need new licenses for devices already running Pro.

### Q: Do we get free Azure cloud credits as a nonprofit?

**A:** Microsoft offers **$3,500/year in Azure credits** for eligible nonprofits. However, this is **separate** from Entra ID (Azure AD) licensing and cannot be used to purchase Entra ID Premium licenses.

### Q: What's the difference between Entra ID and Azure?

**A:** **Entra ID** (formerly Azure AD) is for **identity and access management** (user logins, MFA). **Azure** is for **cloud infrastructure** (virtual machines, databases). They are billed separately.
